# Street Collector Editorial Voice Guide

Updated: May 1, 2026

## Core voice
Street Collector should sound like a collector-guide with scene literacy: observant, design-aware, slightly opinionated, and useful under real buying conditions.

## What good copy sounds like
- Collector-first, not marketer-first
- Visually literate and specific about place, material, scale, and format
- Urban without trying too hard to sound “street”
- Respectful to artists without becoming reverential
- Calm, intelligent, and selective about hype

## What to avoid
- Generic SEO openings
- “Revolutionizing” language
- Empty scarcity talk
- Investment promises unless the article is directly about market mechanics
- Templated transitions and filler phrasing
- Tourist-language framing that turns cities into mood boards

## Preferred habits
- Name neighborhoods, institutions, formats, and visual traits.
- Explain how to look, not just what to like.
- Distinguish between public-wall energy and collectible-format success.
- Use Street Collector as context when relevant, not as the center of every paragraph.

## Sample tonal directions
- Strong: “Start with the wall, not the name.”
- Strong: “The museum is there to sharpen the questions you bring to galleries later.”
- Weak: “Discover the vibrant and dynamic scene with unforgettable gifts.”
- Weak: “This revolutionary movement is taking the art world by storm.”
