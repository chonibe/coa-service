# Street Collector — Artist profile content brief

**For:** writers, researchers, and editors (no Shopify or engineering knowledge required).

**What this is:** Everything you need to **research and draft** one artist’s public profile on the Street Collector site—hero, story, optional cards, exhibitions, press, Instagram notes, and catalogue copy cues.

**After you’re done:** Hand the filled worksheet to whoever enters content in the store. They map fields using the separate technical doc [`artist-profile-content-spec.md`](./artist-profile-content-spec.md).

**Copywriting standard (bios, hooks, quotes, list blurbs):** [`artist-profile-copywriting-playbook.md`](./artist-profile-copywriting-playbook.md) — use this for voice, conversion framing, banned phrases, and **AI agent** instructions. This brief stays the practical worksheet; the playbook is the canonical voice + QA gate.

**Version:** 1.1.0 · **Last updated:** 2026-04-21

---

## Voice & quality

- **Follow the playbook** — [`artist-profile-copywriting-playbook.md`](./artist-profile-copywriting-playbook.md) has the full rules, examples, and mandatory agent checklist.
- **Human and specific.** Prefer concrete places, years, scenes, and quotes over adjectives like “passionate,” “unique,” or “world-renowned.”
- **Facts over hype.** If you’re not sure, flag it—don’t invent exhibitions, quotes, or charity claims.
- **Sources.** Plan on at least: artist interview (or written Q&A), CV or resume, their own site or gallery bio, reputable press, and their Instagram. Use Wikipedia only as a lead; verify anything you take from it.

---

## What appears on the site (so you know why we ask)

- **Top of profile:** Big portrait, their **name**, optional **second line** (real name or subtitle), a **short hook** in italics, and a line like **city · active since year**. Stats (how many editions, how many left) come from the shop automatically—you don’t write those.
- **Overview:** Long **story**, optional **pull quote**, up to **four process images** with short captions, and two optional **callout cards** (community/impact and “only on Street Collector” type messaging).
- **Works:** Product photos and prices come from the shop; you may supply **edition line** copy per piece and **season** names for filters.
- **Exhibitions & press:** Lists you build from CV and research.
- **Instagram:** Official **handle** plus optional **extra images** we’re allowed to use on the page (not the same as their live feed).

## Checklist: what to produce per artist

### 1. Identity & positioning

| Deliverable | What to find out | How to write it |
|-------------|------------------|-----------------|
| **Name as it should appear** | Spelling, accents, how they sign work | Must match how they’re listed in the catalogue |
| **Alias / subtitle** (optional) | Legal name or tagline if different from the main name | Short; appears under the main name |
| **Location line** | Where they live or where the work is rooted | One line, e.g. “Mexico City, Mexico”—avoid vague “global” |
| **Active since** | When they started showing work publicly (murals, shows, documented practice)—not necessarily birth year | e.g. `2015` or `2009–present` |
| **Hero hook** | One line that makes a collector *feel* who they are: origin, tension, or stance—not a mission statement | About **120–180 characters**; tight enough to read in one breath |

### 2. Long-form story (main bio)

| Deliverable | What to cover | How to write it |
|-------------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Body** (about **3–6 short paragraphs**) | Where they’re from, how they started, street/studio practice, relationship to place or community, why editions or prints matter to them, why this collaboration with Street Collector fits | Plain text, short paragraphs, no HTML |
| **Pull quote** | One killer line—ideally something they said, or a line you’d be proud to put on a poster | Emotionally specific; large type on the page |
| **Impact card** (optional) | Workshops, youth programs, % to a cause, community print project—**only if verified** | One short block; skip if unknown |
| **Exclusive card** (optional) | What’s genuinely special about this work **on Street Collector** (first print, format, no reprint elsewhere)—**only if approved by the team** | Factual; no promises legal hasn’t cleared |

### 3. Process images (up to four)

- **What:** Wall in progress, studio, detail, proof, street context—whatever matches the story.
- **For each image:** A **file or URL** the team can publish, plus a **short label** (place and/or year, e.g. “Studio, 2024”).
- **Rights:** Note that the artist (or SC) approves use. If unsure, flag for production.

### 4. Exhibitions & murals

Build a table from the **CV first**, then confirm gaps with the artist.

For each row:

- **Year**
- **Type:** solo show, group show, mural, fair, residency, etc.
- **Title** of the show or project
- **Venue** or commissioner
- **City and country**

Sort with **newest year first**. Do not guess—leave blank or “TBC” if unknown.

### 5. Press & features

For each item:

- **Publication or outlet name**
- **Year**
- **Quote** — exact wording from the piece, or a cleared paraphrase
- **Link** to the article if it’s public (strongly preferred)

### 6. Instagram

- **Handle** of their **official** account (confirm with the artist).
- **Optional:** URLs for **6–12 still images** you’re allowed to use on the profile (campaign shots, exports with permission—not “just screenshot IG” without rights).

### 7. Catalogue copy (coordinate with merch)

- **Edition line** for each print/product (consistent style), e.g. “Ed. 12 / 50”.
- **Season or drop names** for filtering (e.g. Season 1, Season 2)—agree exact wording with the team so tags match.

### 8. You don’t need to supply

- Product **photography**, **prices**, or **stock counts** (merch/ops).
- **“Related artists”** (generated automatically).
