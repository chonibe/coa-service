# Street Collector SEO/GEO Artist Content Guardrails

Last updated: 2026-04-29

## Purpose

This document defines how Street Collector should create SEO and AI-search content without weakening brand trust. The goal is to rank for art-related searches while staying grounded in actual Street Collector inventory, artist profiles, approved artist research, product truth, and collection context.

Use this before drafting public blog posts, category copy, artist cluster pages, AI-search summaries, or editorial guides.

## Source Hierarchy

Use sources in this order. If a claim cannot be supported by these sources, omit it or mark it internally as `TBC`.

1. Current Street Collector storefront pages and product/artist data.

9. `content/artist-research-data.json`
10. Shopify collection descriptions, product descriptions, edition data, and current availability.
11. Artist-owned official sites, CVs, bios, interviews, and approved press links.

Do not treat generic web summaries, unsourced AI output, or competitor pages as publishable source material.

## Brand Truths To Preserve

Every SEO/GEO piece should reinforce these truths:

- Street Collector is a collectible art platform built around the Street Lamp/backlit art lamp and limited edition artworks.
- The collector buys the lamp once and can rotate interchangeable artworks over time.
- The work comes from real independent artists, illustrators, muralists, designers, and contemporary visual artists.
- The experience is about collecting real artist-led editions, not generic wall decor.
- Eligible works include Certificate of Authenticity documentation.
- The site should help collectors understand the artist, edition, object, and trust layer before buying.

Avoid any content that makes Street Collector sound like:

- a generic poster shop
- a stock-image wall art marketplace
- a lighting/decor brand with no artist depth
- an art-history blog disconnected from current inventory
- a publication writing about artists it does not actually carry or contextualize

## Non-Negotiable Trust Rules

- Do not invent exhibitions, murals, shows, clients, awards, press, quotes, edition sizes, sellout claims, charity claims, or exclusivity claims.
- Do not imply Street Collector sells an artist's work unless that artist has a real profile, collection, product, or approved content source.
- Do not write "best artists" or "top artists" lists unless the list is explicitly framed as "artists in the Street Collector collection" or "artists to explore through Street Collector."
- Do not cite press or CVs in public overview prose as a bibliography. Use them to verify facts; write in Street Collector's editorial voice.
- Do not publish artist facts from Instagram alone unless the artist has confirmed them or they are non-sensitive, directly visible, and low-risk.
- Do not publish claims about investment value, resale value, or future appreciation.
- Do not keyword-stuff artist pages or blog posts.
- Do not over-promise AI visibility, rankings, or search placement.

Default stance: shorter and truer beats longer and smoother.

If this table cannot be filled, produce an outline only.

## Free Keyword And Ranking Research Workflow

Use free or low-cost keyword tools to choose topics, but do not let tools override Street Collector relevance. A keyword is only useful if the final article can pull readers back to real Street Collector artists, products, category hubs, or trust content.

### Recommended Free Inputs

| Tool | Use it for | How to use it safely |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Semrush Keyword Magic Tool / free keyword tools | Seed keyword ideas, related questions, keyword difficulty, intent, CPC, broad volume signals | Use as idea generation. Limits and access change, so record the date and do not rely on it as the only source. |
| Google Search Console Performance report | Actual queries where Street Collector already gets impressions, clicks, CTR, and average position | Highest priority once data exists. Queries in positions 4-20 are often the fastest wins. |
| Google Trends | Relative interest, seasonality, regional/country comparison, phrasing comparisons | Use for direction, not absolute volume. Good for comparing "street art prints" vs "urban art prints" or country/city interest. |
| Google autocomplete / People Also Ask | Real phrasing and question angles | Validate against source fit before writing. |
| Competitor SERP review | Search result formats, page types, title patterns, missing angles | Do not copy topics blindly. Find openings that Street Collector can answer with its own artists/products. |

Sources checked on 2026-04-29:

- Semrush Keyword Magic Tool: `https://www.semrush.com/features/keyword-magic-tool/`
- Google Search Console Performance report: `https://support.google.com/webmasters/answer/7576553`
- Google Trends comparison guidance: `https://support.google.com/trends/answer/4359550`

### Keyword Qualification Score

Score each keyword from 0-2. Prioritize topics scoring 8+.

| Factor | 0 | 1 | 2 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Street Collector fit | Generic / unrelated | Related to art, weak product pullback | Directly supports artists, products, lamp, editions, or COA |
| Artist anchor | No current artist | One weak artist tie | 2+ real Street Collector artists with source-backed angles |
| Product path | No product/category path | Can link to shop generally | Clear product/category/collection path |
| Search intent | Vague curiosity | Informational or mixed | Clear collector, buyer, gift, comparison, or trust intent |
| Competition opening | Dominated by stronger authority pages | Some room for a specific angle | Street Collector has a distinctive angle competitors lack |
| Content evidence | Needs unsupported claims | Some claims verified, some TBC | Claims supported by docs, product data, or approved sources |

Do not write a topic only because search volume is high. A lower-volume keyword with a strong artist/product pullback is more valuable than a broad keyword that makes Street Collector sound generic.

### From Keyword To Brief

1. Pull 20-50 keyword ideas from Semrush or another free keyword tool.
2. Pull current query data from Google Search Console.
3. Compare key phrases in Google Trends for seasonality and geography.
4. Group ideas into clusters: object, edition/trust, country/city, style/theme, gift/use case, artist profile.
5. Score each idea with the Keyword Qualification Score.
6. For the best ideas, fill the Blog Brief Template and Country/City Scene Fit block if relevant.
7. Only draft after the brief has artist anchors, internal links, and verified claims.
8. After publishing, monitor Search Console queries and refresh titles/intros for pages sitting in positions 4-20.

## Approved Content Lanes

### Lane 1: Object And Format

Good topics:

- What is a backlit art lamp?
- How backlighting changes color and detail in artwork.
- Why interchangeable art prints work for small spaces.
- How to build a rotating art collection.

Must connect to:

- `/backlit-art-lamp`
- `/interchangeable-art-prints`
- `/shop/products`
- The Street Lamp / Street Collector lamp as the actual display format.

Artist tie-in:

- Use artists whose work has strong color, line, graphic shape, or atmosphere that benefits from illumination.
- Only describe the visual effect in general terms unless there is product imagery or internal proof.

### Lane 2: Collecting And Editions

Good topics:

- How to start collecting street art prints.
- Street art prints vs posters.
- What makes a limited edition print collectible.
- How numbered editions work.
- How to keep art provenance records.

Must connect to:

- `/limited-edition-street-art-prints`
- `/shop/products`
- Product edition details where available.
- COA language only where eligible and consistent with current product data.

Artist tie-in:

- Use current Street Collector artists as examples of styles, cities, processes, and edition context.
- Do not make market-value claims about individual artists.

### Lane 3: Artist Profiles And Entity Building

Good topics:

- Artist profile articles.
- City clusters: Tel Aviv artists, Haifa artists, London artists, Berlin artists, European mural artists.
- Country art-scene guides tied to Street Collector artists: Israeli street art, French illustration, German mural culture, Dutch graphic illustration, Canadian digital art, South African contemporary illustration, etc.
- Style clusters: graphic illustration, surreal urban art, collage, typography, mural-influenced prints.
- "Artists in the Street Collector collection working with..."

Must connect to:

- `/shop/artists/{slug}` where available.
- `/shop/explore-artists`
- The relevant product or collection page.
- Artist research docs and approved bios.

Artist tie-in:

- Every artist paragraph must be grounded in existing profile docs or verified source notes.
- Use the profile playbook voice: concrete places, years, materials, process, and short paragraphs.
- Every country/city art-scene section must include a clear bridge back to specific Street Collector artists from that place or scene.

### Lane 4: Trust Layer

Good topics:

- What is a Certificate of Authenticity for art?
- What should a print certificate include?
- Why provenance matters for new collectors.
- How to document a limited edition print.

Must connect to:

- COA/product truth in current code and docs.
- No legal or resale promises.
- No claim that every product has a COA unless product data confirms it.

Artist tie-in:

- Explain how documentation connects the artwork to artist, title, edition, and platform record.

### Lane 5: Gifts And Use Cases

Good topics:

- Gifts for street art lovers.
- Housewarming gifts for people who hate generic decor.
- Art gifts for designers and visual people.
- First art collection gifts.

Must connect to:

- Actual Street Collector product flow.
- The lamp + swappable print model.
- Artist directory or selected artist examples.

Artist tie-in:

- Recommendations should be framed by taste/style, not unsupported "best" claims.

## Country And City Art-Scene Content Model

Country or city art-scene articles are allowed and useful, but they must not become detached travel/editorial content. Their job is to help search engines and AI systems understand the cultural context around Street Collector artists, then guide collectors back to artists and products.

### Good Uses

- "Tel Aviv Street Artists In The Street Collector Collection"
- "Israeli Street Art And Illustration: Artists To Explore Through Street Collector"
- "French Urban Artists And Illustrators In The Street Collector Collection"
- "German Mural And Graphic Artists To Know"
- "Dutch Illustration And Street-Inspired Prints"
- "London Illustrators And Street Art Gifts"
- "European Mural Artists In The Street Collector Collection"

### Risky Uses

- Broad "history of Israeli art" essays with no Street Collector artist bridge.
- "Best street artists in France" if most artists are not represented by Street Collector.
- Country pages that rank for art tourism but do not help a collector choose an artwork.
- Scene summaries that mention famous unrelated artists more prominently than Street Collector artists.

### Required Structure For Scene Articles

Every country/city scene article should use this structure:

1. Answer-first intro: what the scene is known for and why Street Collector collectors should care.
2. Scene context: 2-4 short paragraphs about the visual culture, city/country, or movement.
3. Street Collector bridge: name the relevant Street Collector artists and why they fit the topic.
4. Artist sections: 3-8 short artist blocks, each with a profile link and source-backed details.
5. Product path: link to the relevant products, artist directory, and category pages.
6. Collector guidance: how to choose a work from this scene or style.
7. FAQ: answer search questions in plain language.

### Internal Link Requirements

Each scene article must include:

- One link to `/shop/explore-artists`.
- At least three links to `/shop/artists/{slug}` when those profiles exist.
- One link to `/shop/products` or a relevant product collection.
- One link to a category hub:
  - `/urban-art-prints`
  - `/limited-edition-street-art-prints`
  - `/backlit-art-lamp`
  - `/interchangeable-art-prints`
- One link to a supporting guide when relevant, for example COA, editions, gifts, or collecting.

### Scene Claim Rules

- Broad scene claims must be general and low-risk unless sourced.
- Use phrases like "Street Collector's collection includes..." instead of "the country's best artists are..."
- If mentioning famous artists outside Street Collector, keep them brief and contextual. The focus should return quickly to artists collectors can explore on Street Collector.
- Never imply a city/country officially endorses Street Collector.
- Never imply every artist from a scene works in street art if their practice is illustration, design, muralism, collage, or studio work.

## Article Draft Rules

Every article should:

- Answer the search query in the first 120 words.
- Mention Street Collector naturally, not as a pasted sales pitch.
- Link to at least one relevant category page.
- Link to at least one artist page or `/shop/explore-artists`.
- Link to `/shop/products` or a relevant product/collection page.
- Use artist examples only from approved current docs.
- Keep artist sections short and specific.
- Include a visible FAQ section.
- Avoid "top 10" framing unless the criteria are explicit and the artists are all part of Street Collector.
- End with a collector action: browse artists, view works, learn about the lamp, compare editions.

Every article should avoid:

- Abstract art-history filler.
- Generic statements like "street art is vibrant and unique."
- Unsupported claims about popularity, rankings, fame, or investment value.
- Artist biographies unrelated to Street Collector.
- Mentioning artists only because they rank well in search.
- Repeating old blog content that is not grounded in the current collection.

## AI Search / GEO Rules

AI providers and answer engines need concise, consistent entity signals. For each public page:

- Use the exact entity name "Street Collector."
- Use consistent product language: "backlit art lamp," "Street Lamp," "limited edition street art prints," "interchangeable art prints."
- Name real artists and link to their profile pages.
- Keep paragraphs answerable and self-contained.
- Avoid ambiguity around whether Street Collector is a blog, a gallery, a marketplace, or a lamp brand. It is an artist-led collecting platform built around a lamp/display system and editioned prints.
- Do not publish broad "history of street art" pages unless they directly help collectors understand Street Collector artists or products.

## Editorial Workflow

1. Pick keyword lane and article topic.
2. Fill the Blog Brief Template.
3. Pull artist facts from approved docs.
4. Verify live artist/product availability.
5. Draft in Street Collector voice.
6. Run the Brand Risk Check.
7. Add internal links and FAQ.
8. Review for factual claims, rights, COA language, and product truth.
9. Publish only after all `TBC` items are removed or omitted.
10. Track Search Console queries and update article intros/titles after data appears.

## Stop Conditions

Do not draft or publish if:

- The article cannot name a Street Collector category, artist, or product.
- Artist claims are sourced only from memory or AI output.
- The post would rank for a query but not help a collector understand Street Collector.
- It depends on unavailable products but implies they are available.
- It uses copyrighted images without rights.
- It would confuse the distinction between artist practice, product format, and platform trust layer.
