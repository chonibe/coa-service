# Apollo Manual Prospecting Playbook - Street Collector

Goal: find the right buyer for investor / executive gifting without doing anything that looks like automation abuse.

## Who To Prospect First

Use this order inside each company:

1. Chief of Staff / CEO Office / Executive Operations
2. Corporate Development / Investor Relations / Strategic Finance
3. COO / Business Operations / Head of Operations
4. VP Brand / Head of Brand / Brand Partnerships
5. Head of Events / Community / Ecosystem / Partnerships

Avoid as first contacts:

- CEO, unless the company is clearly founder-led and under roughly 250 employees.
- SDR, BDR, Account Executive, Sales, Demand Gen, Recruiter, HR generalist.
- Creators, ambassadors, influencers, advisors, investors, consultants, freelancers.

## Apollo-Safe Operating Rules

- Work manually in batches of 10 companies or fewer.
- Reveal at most 1-2 emails per company.
- Pause after 20-30 email reveals in a session.
- Do not use repeated rapid clicks on `Access email`.
- Do not run broad searches like "all tech companies" or "all people with COO".
- Prefer company-domain filters plus title filters.
- Do not request mobile numbers for now.
- Do not create sequences or send from Apollo until the list is cleaned.
- If Cloudflare appears, stop automation and solve manually.

## What To Save

Save the selected contact to an Apollo list named:

`Street Collector - Creator Investor Gifts`

Copy these fields into our local CSV after each reveal:

- Company
- Domain
- Contact name
- Title
- Email
- Location
- Apollo email status
- Why this person
- Priority: A, B, or C

Priority rules:

- A: CEO-office, corporate development, investor relations, COO, head of ops, brand partnerships/events leader.
- B: senior brand, community, ecosystem, partnerships, executive business partner.
- C: marketing/growth/revenue roles, or strong company fit but weak title.
